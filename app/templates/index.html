This website aims to provide information on mass shootings in the United States from 2017 to 2022. The dashboards on this site will display the counts of shootings incidents, victims killed, and victims injured. It is important to note that the definition of mass shootings may differ across other platforms. The definition used on this site states that if 4 or more people are killed then it is considered as a mass killing.



At a Glance


Since 2020, the number of shooting incidents and victims have been on the rise.


A majority of shooting victims were white and/or in their 20s to 30s.



A majority of shooting offenders were male and/or in their 20s.


Most incidents occurred in residences.


Most victims were killed in residences and injured in open spaces.


The most common weapons used in mass shootings were
semiautomatic rifles and semiautomatic handguns.
